### Importance

---

## Interviewer's Intent

The interviewer wants to:

- Evaluate whether you think in terms of outcomes rather than features
- Assess product and platform ownership
- Understand how you measure engineering productivity
- Verify business impact
- Determine whether the initiative delivers real value

---

## Recommended Answer (English)

I believe the success of NAtlas and NStack should be measured by improvements in engineering effectiveness rather than by the amount of documentation generated.

There are several metrics I would consider important.

First, onboarding efficiency.

If a new engineer can understand a project, its history, and key architectural decisions significantly faster, that is a strong indicator that knowledge is being preserved effectively.

Second, knowledge discovery.

Engineers should be able to quickly find previous design decisions, implementation examples, and solutions to similar problems without relying on tribal knowledge.

Third, reduction of duplicate work.

Teams often spend time solving problems that have already been solved elsewhere in the organization. If engineers can discover previous work more easily, duplicate effort should decrease.

Fourth, engineering productivity.

If teams spend less time searching for information, understanding legacy systems, or reverse-engineering code, they can spend more time delivering value.

Finally, AI effectiveness.

As more organizational knowledge becomes accessible to LLMs through MCP integration, AI systems can provide more accurate and context-aware assistance.

Ultimately, the goal is to reduce the cost of knowledge retrieval while improving engineering velocity, collaboration, and long-term maintainability.
